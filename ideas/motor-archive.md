# Motor Archive ⭐ Top Choice

Buying a used car is genuinely stressful. You're spending thousands 
of dollars on something you can't fully inspect, from someone who 
wants to sell it. The tools people use, Carfax, KBB, Edmunds, all 
give you data. None of them tell you what to do with it.

Carfax says this car had one accident. Is that bad? Depends on the 
car, the damage, and the mileage. KBB gives you a price range. Is 
$49k fair for a 2017 BMW M3 at 58k miles? Depends on what's about 
to break on that specific engine at that specific mileage. Nobody 
tells you that part.

Motor Archive has two modes.

**Buy Mode** — you paste a VIN or listing URL. The app pulls recall 
history, owner complaints, and known failure patterns for that exact 
model at that mileage. Then it gives you a plain-English verdict: 
is this a reasonable buy, a risky one, or overpriced given what's 
likely coming? It also generates a pre-purchase checklist specific 
to that car, not a generic "check the tires" list.

**Own Mode** — you've already bought the car. Check engine light 
comes on. You enter the error code or describe the symptom, and the 
app tells you what it usually means on your specific car, what it 
typically costs to fix, and what to say to the mechanic so you don't 
get overcharged.

The insight behind it: every existing car tool is built for one 
moment, the purchase, or the repair, or the price check. Nobody 
built the one that stays useful after you drive off the lot.

## Data
- NHTSA API, free US government data on recalls, complaints, 
  and safety ratings going back decades
- Anthropic API, synthesizes the raw data into plain-English 
  verdicts and recommendations
